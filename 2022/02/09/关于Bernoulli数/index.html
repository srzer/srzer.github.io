我们从坚果剥出时间并教它走路.

关于Bernoulli数

定义Bernoulli数$B_n$为
$$
\frac{x}{e^x-1}=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{B_ix^i}{i!}
$$
可得出Bernulli数与tanx的关系:
$$
\frac{x}{e^x-1}+\frac{x}{2}=\frac{x}{2}\frac{e^x+1}{e^x-1}\
=\frac{x}{2}coth(\frac{x}{2})\
则xcoth(x)=\frac{2x}{e^{2x}-1}+x\
=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{B_i(2x)^i}{i!}+x\
显然左式为偶函数,则只取右式偶次幂\
xcoth(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{B_{2i}(2x)^{2i}}{(2i)!}\
又知icoth(ix)=cot(x)\
cot(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{B_{2i}2^{2i}(-1)^ix^{2i-1}}{(2i)!}\
又有tanx=cotx-2cot(2x)\
tanx=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{B_{2i}(2^{2i}-1)2^{2i}(-1)^{i-1}x^{2i-1}}{(2i)!}
$$
再从复分析的角度出发

Cauchy’s Residue Th.

Let P be a simple closed curve (counter-clockwise).Let f be differntiable everywhere inside (and on) the curve, except for a finite number of pole sigularities: $z_j$ with residue $r_j$.
$$
\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_Pf(z)dz=\sum_{j=1}^mr_j
$$

$$
tan(z) = \frac{sinz}{cosz}\
=\frac{1}{i}\frac{e^{iz}-e^{-iz}}{e^{iz}+e^{-iz}}\
=\frac{1}{i}(1-\frac{2}{e^{2iz}+1})
$$
Obviously, $\frac{tan(z)}{z^{n+1}}$ have poles in

  • $z=0$
  • $z=(m+\frac{1}{2})\pi$

Assume $tan(z)=\sum_{k\ge0}b_kx^k$

Let P be a symmetric square,eg.

$z=(-)m\pi+iy,y\in[-m\pi,+m\pi]$ and $z=x+i(-)m\pi,x\in[-m\pi,+m\pi]$

So we have
$$
\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_P\frac{tan(z)}{z^{n+1}}dz=\sum r_j\
=b_n+\sum \frac{(r_{tan(z)})j}{z_j^{n+1}}
$$
It’s easy to see that $r{tan(z)}=-1$.
$$
\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_P\frac{tan(z)}{z^{n+1}}dz=b_n-\sum_{(j+\frac{1}{2}\pi)insideP}\frac{1}{(j+\frac{1}{2}\pi)^{n+1}}
$$
It’s also easy to see that for any $z\in P$, $|tan(z)|<5$.

Let $R_m \rightarrow \infin$
$$
|\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_P\frac{tan(z)}{z^{n+1}}dz|<\frac{1}{2\pi i}5\frac{4R_m}{R_m^{n+1}}\rightarrow 0
$$

$$
b_n=\sum_m\frac{1}{((m+\frac{1}{2})\pi)^{n+1}}
$$
We know in advance that If $n\in 2Z$, $b_n$=0.

When $n\in 2Z-1$, we have
$$
b_n=2\sum_{m\ge0}\frac{1}{((m+\frac{1}{2})\pi)^{n+1}}\
= 2(\frac{2}{\pi})^{n+1}\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{1}{(2i+1)^{n+1}}
$$

$$
b_{2i-1}=2\sum_{m\ge0}\frac{1}{((m+\frac{1}{2})\pi)^{2i}}\
=2(\frac{2}{\pi})^{2i}\sum_{m=0}^{+\infin}\frac{1}{(2m+1)^{2i}}
$$
所以我们有如下结果
$$
tan(x)=\sum_{i=1}^{+\infin}2(\frac{2}{\pi})^{2i}\sum_{m=0}^{+\infin}\frac{1}{(2m+1)^{2i}}x^{2i-1}
$$

$$
\frac{B_{2i}(2^{2i}-1)2^{2i}(-1)^{i-1}}{(2i)!}=2(\frac{2}{\pi})^{2i}\sum_{m=0}^{+\infin}\frac{1}{(2m+1)^{2i}}
$$
特别地,可以推出
$$
\sum_{i=1}\frac{1}{i^2}=\frac{6}{\pi^2}
$$
接下来再谈谈Bernoulli数与等幂和的关系
$$
S_m(n)=\sum_{i=1}^ni^m
$$
考虑生成函数
$$
f(x)=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}S_i(n)\frac{x^i}{i!}\
=e^x\frac{e^{nx}-1}{e^x-1}\
=\frac{e^{nx}-1}{x}(x+\frac{x}{e^x-1})\
=(\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{n^{i+1}}{(i+1)!}x^i)(x+\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}B_i\frac{x^i}{i!})\
=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{n^{i+1}}{(i+1)!}x^i\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}(-1)^iB_i\frac{x^i}{i!}\
=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}x^i\sum_{j=0}^{i}\frac{n^{j+1}(-1)^{i-j}B_{i-j}}{(j+1)!(i-j)!}\
=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{x^i}{(i+1)!}\sum_{j=0}^iC_{i+1}^{j+1}(-1)^{i-j}B_{i-j}n^{j+1}
$$
即
$$
S_m(n)=\frac{1}{m+1}\sum_{r=0}^mC_{m+1}^{r+1}(-1)^{m-r}B_{m-r}n^{r+1}
$$
关于Bernoulli数,就说这么多吧,累死俺了.

额,再补充一下Bernoulli数的递推性.
$$
\frac{x}{e^x-1}=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}B_i\frac{x^i}{i!}\
则有\
x+\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}B_i\frac{x^i}{i!}=e^x\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}B_i\frac{x^i}{i!}\
=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\frac{x^i}{i!}\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}B_i\frac{x^i}{i!}\
=\sum_{i=0}^{+\infin}\sum_{j=0}^{i}\frac{B_{i-j}x^i}{j!(i-j)!}\
那么i\ge2时,我们有:\
\frac{B_i}{i!}=\sum_{j=0}^i\frac{B_j}{j!(i-j)!}\
即\
B_i=\sum_{j=0}^iC_i^jBj\
即0=\sum_{m=0}^{n-1}C_n^mB_m
$$
